# Platform Playbook

Use this file when deciding where to search, what to capture, and how to judge platform-specific material quality.

## Search intent by platform

### Weibo

Best for:

- hot-search proof
- public reaction
- creator or KOL statements
- timeline screenshots that show discussion density

Good capture targets:

- hot-search pages
- search result pages
- individual post pages
- verified account posts

Watch for:

- login walls
- visitor throttling
- noisy sidebars
- screenshots where the useful text is too small on mobile layouts

Use when the script needs:

- "everyone is talking about this"
- controversy
- reactions
- public sentiment

### Xiaohongshu

Best for:

- consumer-facing reactions
- creator notes
- trend-style discussion
- aesthetic screenshots with social proof

Good capture targets:

- search result pages
- individual note pages
- account pages when the creator identity matters

Watch for:

- aggressive login prompts
- dense comment layouts
- oversized overlays
- note pages that look polished but say very little

Use when the script needs:

- relatable user perspective
- trend proof
- softer lifestyle framing

### Bilibili

Best for:

- tutorials
- demos
- creator explainers
- community proof via search results or ranking pages

Good capture targets:

- search result pages
- video pages
- chapter markers
- subtitle-backed sections

Watch for:

- title-clickbait mismatch
- long intros before the useful part
- low-resolution uploads
- videos that are relevant only at a very high level

Use when the script needs:

- feature demos
- creator explanation
- domestic proof of adoption

### News platforms

Best for:

- credibility
- policy or industry context
- risk reminders
- external validation

Good capture targets:

- clean article headers
- result aggregation pages
- screenshots with visible outlet and date

Watch for:

- ad-heavy article pages
- syndication copies with poor layout
- headlines that overstate the claim

Use when the script needs:

- authority
- sober framing
- evidence rather than hype

### Official sites and docs

Best for:

- concept definition
- feature explanation
- product workflow screenshots
- capability confirmation

Good capture targets:

- homepages
- docs pages
- feature pages
- product screenshots

Watch for:

- marketing-only visuals with no usable information
- giant hero sections that look nice but say little

Use when the script needs:

- "what this thing is"
- "what it can actually do"

### GitHub and open community pages

Best for:

- ecosystem proof
- repo activity
- star count and community traction
- open-source reference

Good capture targets:

- repo overview pages
- release pages
- issue or discussion pages when the discussion itself matters

Watch for:

- stale repos
- community arguments that are too technical for the edit

Use when the script needs:

- traction
- developer credibility
- ecosystem scale

### Overseas news and communities

Best for:

- global perspective
- outside-China validation
- debate, criticism, or enthusiasm abroad

Good capture targets:

- article headers
- discussion threads
- screenshots with clear identity and context

Watch for:

- culture-specific jokes that will not land in the edit
- text walls that are unreadable in short-form video

Use when the script needs:

- international perspective
- "this is not only a local topic"

### Stock and reusable B-roll sources

Best for:

- pacing shots
- atmosphere
- transitions
- clean filler that supports the story

Good capture targets:

- desktop work scenes
- typing, coding, office, browser, server, dashboard visuals

Watch for:

- generic visuals with no narrative support
- overusing filler where evidence is required

Use when the script needs:

- rhythm
- transition
- visual breathing room

## Platform choice rules

When choosing where to search first:

1. Start from the strongest evidence source for the segment.
2. Move to creator or community sources if you need emotion or reaction.
3. Add stock or atmosphere only after evidence and explanation are covered.

## Capture rules

Prefer screenshots or video clips that:

- have one obvious focal point
- can be understood quickly
- contain visible context such as source, date, or platform identity when credibility matters
- do not require the viewer to read tiny text for three seconds
